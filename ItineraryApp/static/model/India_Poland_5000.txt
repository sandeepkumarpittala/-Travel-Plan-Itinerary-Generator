**IV. Detailed Itinerary:**

**Day 1: Arrival in Warsaw & Old Town Exploration**

*   **Morning:** Arrive at Warsaw Chopin Airport (WAW). Take a train or bus to your hotel in the city center.
*   **Afternoon:** Check into your hotel and leave your luggage.
*   **Afternoon/Evening:** Explore Warsaw's Old Town (Stare Miasto), a UNESCO World Heritage site.  Visit the Royal Castle, Market Square, and St. John's Archcathedral.  Enjoy a traditional Polish dinner at a restaurant in the Old Town.
*   **Hotel:** Hotel Metropol, Warsaw

**Day 2: Warsaw: History & Culture**

*   **Morning:** Visit the Warsaw Uprising Museum, a moving tribute to the city's resistance during World War II.
*   **Afternoon:** Explore the POLIN Museum of the History of Polish Jews, an architectural masterpiece that tells the story of Jewish life in Poland.
*   **Evening:** Attend a classical music concert or a traditional Polish folk performance.  Enjoy dinner at a restaurant in the Nowy Swiat (New World Street) area.
*   **Hotel:** Hotel Metropol, Warsaw

**Day 3: Travel to Krakow & Krakow Old Town**

*   **Morning:** Take an early morning train from Warsaw to Krakow (approx. 2.5-3 hours). Book train tickets in advance through the PKP Intercity website.
*   **Afternoon:** Check into your hotel in Krakow.
*   **Afternoon/Evening:** Explore Krakow's Main Market Square (Rynek Glowny), one of the largest medieval squares in Europe. Visit St. Mary's Basilica, the Cloth Hall (Sukiennice), and Wawel Castle. Enjoy dinner in the Old Town.
*   **Hotel:** Hotel Wyspianski, Krakow

**Day 4: Auschwitz-Birkenau Memorial & Museum**

*   **Morning:** Take a guided tour to Auschwitz-Birkenau Memorial and Museum, a former Nazi concentration camp (approx. 1.5-hour drive from Krakow).  Book your tour in advance.  This is a somber but important historical site.
*   **Afternoon:** Reflect on your experience and return to Krakow.
*   **Evening:** Enjoy a traditional Polish meal in the Kazimierz (Jewish Quarter) district.
*   **Hotel:** Hotel Wyspianski, Krakow

**Day 5: Krakow: Kazimierz & Salt Mine**

*   **Morning:** Explore Kazimierz, Krakow's historic Jewish Quarter. Visit synagogues, art galleries, and enjoy the unique atmosphere.
*   **Afternoon:** Take a trip to the Wieliczka Salt Mine, a UNESCO World Heritage site. Explore the underground chambers, chapels, and salt sculptures.
*   **Evening:** Enjoy live music in one of Kazimierz's many bars and restaurants.
*   **Hotel:** Hotel Wyspianski, Krakow

**Day 6: Travel to Gdansk & Gdansk Old Town**

*   **Morning:** Take a morning train from Krakow to Gdansk (approx. 5-6 hours).
*   **Afternoon:** Check into your hotel in Gdansk.
*   **Afternoon/Evening:** Explore Gdansk's Old Town, including Dluga Street (Long Street), the Neptune Fountain, and St. Mary's Church (the largest brick church in the world). Enjoy dinner overlooking the Motlawa River.
*   **Hotel:** Hotel Mercure Gdansk Stare Miasto, Gdansk

**Day 7: Gdansk: Maritime History & Solidarity**

*   **Morning:** Visit the National Maritime Museum in Gdansk, showcasing Poland's maritime history.
*   **Afternoon:** Explore the European Solidarity Centre, a museum dedicated to the Solidarity movement that played a key role in the fall of communism.
*   **Evening:** Take a stroll along the Motlawa River and enjoy dinner at a seafood restaurant.
*   **Hotel:** Hotel Mercure Gdansk Stare Miasto, Gdansk

**Day 8: Sopot & Gdynia (Optional Day Trip)**

*   **Day Trip:** Take a short train ride to Sopot, a popular seaside resort town, and Gdynia, a modern port city.  Enjoy the beaches, piers, and waterfront promenades.
*   **Evening:** Return to Gdansk for dinner.  Alternatively, enjoy dinner in Sopot for a different atmosphere.
*   **Hotel:** Hotel Mercure Gdansk Stare Miasto, Gdansk

**Day 9: Malbork Castle**

*   **Day Trip:** Take a day trip to Malbork Castle, a UNESCO World Heritage site and the largest castle in the world measured by land area. This imposing Teutonic castle is a must-see for history buffs.
*   **Evening:** Return to Gdansk and enjoy a farewell Polish dinner.
*   **Hotel:** Hotel Mercure Gdansk Stare Miasto, Gdansk

**Day 10: Departure**

*   **Morning:** Enjoy a final Polish breakfast.
*   **Morning/Afternoon:** Depending on your flight schedule, do some last-minute souvenir shopping or visit any attractions you missed.
*   **Afternoon/Evening:** Transfer to Gdansk Lech Walesa Airport (GDN) for your flight back to India.

**VI. Tips for Saving Money:**

*   **Travel in the Shoulder Season:** April-May or September-October offer better prices and fewer crowds.
*   **Book Flights and Accommodation in Advance:** This is crucial for securing the best deals.
*   **Use Public Transport:** Trains and buses are efficient and affordable.
*   **Eat at Local Restaurants and Street Food Stalls:**  Avoid tourist traps.
*   **Take Advantage of Free Activities:** Many museums offer free admission on certain days. Explore parks and gardens.
*   **Consider a City Pass:** If you plan to visit many attractions, a city pass might save you money.
*   **Cook Some of Your Own Meals:** If your accommodation has a kitchenette, consider buying groceries and preparing some meals.
*   **Look for Free Walking Tours:**  These are a great way to get an overview of a city.
*   **Consider Hostels or Guesthouses:** These are often cheaper than hotels.

**VII. Important Notes:**

*   **Visa:**  Indian citizens require a Schengen visa to enter Poland.  Apply well in advance of your trip.
*   **Currency:** Polish Zloty (PLN).  It's best to exchange currency upon arrival in Poland or use ATMs.
*   **Language:** Polish.  English is spoken in tourist areas.
*   **Safety:** Poland is a generally safe country.  Be aware of your surroundings and take precautions against petty theft.
*   **Travel Insurance:**  Highly recommended.
*   **Adaptor:** You will likely need a European adapter for your electronics.
